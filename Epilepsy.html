Epilepsy

A disorder in which nerve cell activity in the brain is disturbed, causing seizures.

What Causes Epilepsy

Epilepsy may occur as a result of a genetic disorder or an acquired brain injury, such as a trauma or stroke.

What happens to the brain when a patient has epilepsy?

In epilepsy the brain's electrical rhythms have a tendency to become imbalanced, resulting in recurrent seizures. In patients with seizures, the normal electrical pattern is disrupted by sudden and synchronized bursts of electrical energy that may briefly affect their consciousness, movements or sensations.

Symptoms

  • Fainting
  • Seizure
  • Muscle spasm
  • Amnesia
  • Anxiety
  • Depression
  • Temporary paralysis after a seizure

Treatment

Medication-A patient can take nerve pain medication such as Sedative and Anticonvulsant
