Tumor in the Brain

A mass or growth of abnormal cells in your brain

What Causes a Tumor

Mutations or defects in genes may cause cells in the brain to grow uncontrollably, causing a tumor. The only known environmental cause of brain tumors is having exposure to large amounts of radiation from X-rays or previous cancer treatments.

How does a brain tumor develop?

A brain tumor can form in the brain cells, or it can begin elsewhere and spread to the brain. As the tumor grows, it creates pressure on and changes the function of surrounding brain tissue, which causes signs and symptoms such as headaches, nausea and balance problems.

Symptoms

  • Strong headache
  • Blurred vision
  • Loss of balance
  • Confusion
  • Seizures
  • Some cases may have no symptoms

Treatment

  • Medication- Chemotherapy
  • Surgery- Craniotomy
  • Medical process- Tomotherapy and Radiation Therapy
